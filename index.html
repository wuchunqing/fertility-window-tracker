How it works

We estimate ovulation as your cycle length minus 14 days (luteal phase rule). The fertile window spans 5 days before ovulation through 1 day after, when sperm and egg can meet. Irregular cycles widen the uncertainty band — combine with OPK tests for precision.

Important note

This tracker uses population averages, not your unique hormones. PCOS, stress, travel, and illness can shift ovulation. Always discuss conception planning with your OB/GYN or fertility specialist.

FAQ

Why is the fertile window 6–7 days, not just ovulation day?

Sperm live up to 5 days in the reproductive tract, while the egg is viable for only 12–24 hours after release. Having intercourse before ovulation lets sperm wait for the egg.

How accurate is LMP-based tracking?

For regular 26–32 day cycles, estimates are reasonably close. Irregular cycles benefit from ovulation predictor kits (OPK) or basal body temperature (BBT) charts.

When should we have intercourse?

Every other day during the fertile window maintains sperm quality without depletion. The 2 days before ovulation typically offer the highest conception probability.

When should I see a fertility specialist?

Under 35: after 12 months of trying. Age 35+: after 6 months. Sooner if cycles are very irregular, painful, or absent.
